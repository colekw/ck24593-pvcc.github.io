U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ)

"The Justice Department's mission is to uphold the rule of law, keep our country safe, and protect civil rights. Our work across the Department-including all 94 U.S. Attorneys' offices, our law enforcement agencies, grantmaking components and litigating divisions-is guided by these three co-equal priorities."

The Department of Justice (DOJ) is responsible for enforcing federal laws within the United States. It is led by the Attorney General who is appointed by the President, along with around 100,000 attorneys, agents, and other staff. They provide legal advice to the President's Cabinet and represent the United States in criminal and civil litigation.

Sub-Agencies of the DOJ:

  • Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms, and Explosives Bureau (ATF): Protects the public from crimes involving firearms, explosives, etc. Also regulates firearm and explosives commerce.
  • Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA): Enforces controlled substance laws and regulations.
  • Executive Office for Immigration Review: Conducts immigration hearings, reviews all in a fair manner.
  • Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI): Investigates crimes and works with others to combat threats. Agents do field work, investigative work, and many other different tasks.

U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS)

"The Department of Homeland Security has a vital mission: to secure the nation from the many threats we face. This requires the hard work of more than 260,000 employees in jobs that range from aviation and border security to emergency response, from cybersecurity analyst to chemical facility inspector. Our duties are wide-ranging, and our goal is clear - keeping America safe."

DHS helps our country by helping after a natural disaster, protecting our economy, U.S. border security, stopping terrorism, identifying threats, and securing cyberspace.

Branches of the DHS:

  • U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services: Determines eligibility for citizenship, reviews naturalization applications, and hosts ceremonies for immigrants.
  • U.S. Coast Guard: Protects the sea, enforces maritime law & safety, transports commercial vessels, and acts as a safeguard.
  • Customs and Border Protection: Prevents dangerous individuals, weapons, drugs, and other items from entering through borders.
  • Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security: Builds a more secure infrastructure to decrease the risk of cyber attacks and protect valuable information.
  • Secret Service: Special Agents are assigned to protect valuable assets, conduct investigations, and plan security for special events.
  • Transportation Security Administration (TSA): Provides security and protection for all travelers, preventing dangerous people and items from boarding flights.
